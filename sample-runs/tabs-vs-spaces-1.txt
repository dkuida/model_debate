# Tabs Are Objectively Superior — Let Me Explain Why

The core argument for tabs is **semantic correctness and flexibility**. A tab character *means* indentation — that's its entire purpose for existing. When you use a tab, you're saying "this is one level of indentation," and every developer on your team can render that however they prefer: 2 spaces wide, 4 spaces wide, 8 spaces wide. Their editor, their choice. Spaces force your *aesthetic preference* onto every single person who touches your code. That's not collaboration, that's tyranny with a spacebar.

Tabs also win on **accessibility grounds**, which is an argument spaces-advocates conveniently ignore. Developers with visual impairments or reading difficulties like dyslexia often need wider indentation to parse code structure clearly. With tabs, they set their editor to whatever width works for their brain. With spaces, they're stuck with whatever the original author decided looked pretty on *their* monitor. Furthermore, tabs are literally more efficient — one character versus two, four, or eight. Over a large codebase, that's real file size savings and fewer keystrokes.

The *only* credible argument for spaces is **consistency across environments** — what you see is what everyone sees. But this is a tooling problem disguised as a philosophy, and modern editors solved it years ago with EditorConfig and standardized rendering. Spaces win arguments in the short term by hiding the real issue. Tabs win in the long term by respecting developer autonomy. The debate isn't even close — tabs are the principled choice.
